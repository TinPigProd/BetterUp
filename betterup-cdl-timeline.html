Weeks 1 to 4
Customer experience
BetterUp Delivery
What we are mapping
Who in the organization has formal authority over this program and who has informal influence. Who are the champions and who are the skeptics. Where the resistance will come from and what is underneath it. This map shapes every communication and cascade path design.
Client Delivery Director + Change Partner
What readiness means here
Not whether the client is excited about the program. Whether the organizational environment will hold what participants learn. Are managers prepared to reinforce courageous leadership behaviors? Is there psychological safety present, or are we asking people to rumble with vulnerability in a place that punishes it?
Prosci ADKAR connection
Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement. The readiness assessment maps where the organization sits on each dimension before the first session begins.
Client Delivery Director + Change Partner
What the sponsor needs to say and when
The launch only works if the executive sponsor shows up with credibility before the first cohort session. This briefing equips them: the right language, the right timing, how to signal that this is a leadership investment not an HR program. The Change Partner drafts ghostwritten messages and talking points. The Client Delivery Director ensures they land with the right relational context.
Change Partner + Client Delivery Director
What gets built here
End-to-end change management strategy for the program launch. Cascade path design identifying champions and skeptics at every level. Communications strategy that does not just deliver messages but shapes the story that makes change possible. Manager toolkits and talking points so frontline leaders can act as multipliers of the Dare to Lead transformation.
Owner
Change Partner
Technical scope
BetterUp platform configured for the client's cohort structure, user provisioning aligned to participant list, SSO or access method confirmed with client IT. This is where the implementation team engages directly with the client's technical contacts.
Implementation + IT
What must be confirmed
Participant access verified. SSO working. Data flows confirmed between client systems and BetterUp platform. Notification pathways tested. This gate does not clear until a real participant account completes a real end-to-end test, not a technical checklist.
Why this is a gate
A launch that fails technically in the first week destroys the trust the client came to build. This gate protects the client relationship before it has had a chance to form.
Implementation + Client IT
What facilitators need to know
Industry context. Organizational culture signals from the readiness assessment. Who the participants are and what pressures they are under. Where the likely resistance will appear and how to hold it without softening the methodology. Facilitators do not walk into a room cold.
Client Delivery Director + Facilitation Lead
What must be true before this clears
Executive sponsor is visible and briefed. Manager reinforcement plan exists. Participant communication has gone out with the right framing. If the organizational environment is not ready to hold the work, this gate creates the moment to name that with the client rather than proceeding and failing quietly.

Customer experience
BetterUp Delivery
What makes this land
The sponsor does not read a prepared statement. They speak to why this work matters to them personally. That requires the Client Delivery Director to have had the right pre-brief conversation, not just sent a talking points document.
Client Delivery Director + Executive Sponsor
Why this is first
The Dare to Lead curriculum is sequenced deliberately. Vulnerability is the foundation. Everything that follows, trust, values, resilience, depends on participants having engaged honestly with this module. Skipping it or softening it at client request is the most common form of methodology drift. It does not happen.
Facilitation Lead
What managers receive and when
Within 48 hours of Session 1, the managers of participants receive language, conversation starters, and context to reinforce what their people are learning. The Change Partner builds the toolkit. The Client Delivery Director ensures it reaches the right people through the right channels. If managers are not activated at launch, the organizational environment works against the program rather than with it.
Change Partner + Client Delivery Director
What gets surfaced here
Energy in the room. Where armor was visible. What landed and what did not. Any dynamics in the cohort that need attention. Any signs that the organizational environment is working against the program. This debrief happens within 24 hours of every session, not at the end of the program.
Client Delivery Director + Facilitation Lead
What the synthesis surfaces
Participant reflections are read against the four skill sets. Where is strong signal present? Where is resistance showing up? Is the cohort engaging with vulnerability or performing engagement? This first synthesis creates the baseline the rest of the program is measured against.
Client Delivery Director
What this gate confirms
Platform working. Participants engaged. Manager cascade active. No critical methodology drift in Session 1. Facilitation quality confirmed. If any of these conditions is not met, the delivery team addresses it before Session 2, not after the program closes.

Customer experience
BetterUp Delivery
What this conversation requires
The Client Delivery Director tells the sponsor what the cohort data actually shows, including things that are uncomfortable. Leaders who were brave enough to buy Dare to Lead deserve a delivery partner brave enough to tell them the truth. This is where the mid-program value narrative is delivered and adjusted.
Client Delivery Director + Executive Sponsor
The methodology protection principle
Clients will ask to compress the timeline, skip modules, or soften the language. Each request has a logic to it. The response is never a flat no. It is a conversation about what the client is actually trying to solve, followed by a clear explanation of what adaptation is possible and what is not. Where communications or messaging adaptations are warranted, the Change Partner designs the adjusted approach while the Client Delivery Director holds the methodology line.
Client Delivery Director + Change Partner
What the Change Partner owns in sustain
Real-time signal from participant reflections, manager feedback, and organizational dynamics feeds into the Change Partner's ongoing communications refinement. Mid-program messaging interventions keep the sponsor visible and participants oriented. Listening loops close within 48 hours of each session so the next communication is shaped by what actually happened, not what was planned.
Owner
Change Partner
What gets reviewed
Facilitation quality signals. Participant engagement trajectory. Risk register updated. Any methodology drift identified and addressed before it compounds. Open items assigned and tracked. The health check is short and specific. It is not a status meeting.
Client Delivery Director + CS + Facilitation Lead
What changes based on this signal
Facilitation approach adjusted where needed. Client communications calibrated to what participants are actually experiencing. Early indicators of disengagement addressed before they become patterns. The signal loop closes within 48 hours of each session.
Client Delivery Director
What is checked
All four skill set modules are being delivered as designed. No sessions have been compressed below minimum time requirements. Facilitation is holding the curriculum rather than softening it. Any deviations are documented with the rationale and approved by the delivery lead before they happen, not after.

Customer experience
BetterUp Delivery
What the narrative connects
Not a program completion report. A story that connects what participants experienced to what the business is trying to accomplish. The how of how leaders showed up connects to the what of what their teams produced. The sponsor needs to be able to stand up in front of their leadership team and justify the investment. This narrative makes that possible.
Client Delivery Director + People Insights
What this conversation opens
This conversation with the sponsor names what the work looks like as it deepens: additional cohorts, new leadership levels brought into the program, manager reinforcement structures, integration with performance systems, and sustained coaching. The Dare to Lead methodology does not have a finish line. The engagement evolves as the organization does.
Client Delivery Director + CS
What gets documented
What worked in the delivery model and what needs to change. What the client taught us about this industry, this culture, this kind of organizational challenge. What the facilitation team learned about holding the Dare to Lead curriculum under real enterprise pressure. This learning goes back into the delivery infrastructure.
Client Delivery Director + Full Team
What gets built from what was learned
What enterprise clients consistently push back on. Where the methodology is hardest to hold. What organizational patterns emerge across cohorts. The Change Partner documents what great communications and change strategy looked like in this engagement and builds it into reusable frameworks, cascade templates, and executive talking point guides that raise the standard for every engagement that follows.

The work transforms.

The Elevate phase is not a conclusion. It is a transition. Courageous cultures are built over time, not installed in a series of sessions. The engagement evolves as the organization expands the work to new cohorts, new leadership levels, and new organizational challenges. What gets built in the first engagement becomes the foundation for what comes next. The timeline does not end. It compounds.
